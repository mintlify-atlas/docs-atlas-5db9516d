## What We're Looking For

NDZ Journal publishes essays and articles that help readers **take action, build skills, and live more focused lives**. We're looking for content that:

- Challenges conventional wisdom with fresh perspectives
- Provides actionable frameworks readers can apply immediately
- Shares honest experiences, including failures and lessons learned
- Respects the reader's intelligence and time

<Note>
  Remember: "Less is More." Every word should earn its place.
</Note>

## Article Structure

### 1. Hook with a Story or Question

Most NDZ articles open with:
- A relatable personal experience
- A provocative question
- A scenario readers can see themselves in

**Example from action-faking.md:10-14**:
> "In about 10 minutes, I finished watching a thought-provoking motivational piece that advised me to get to the grind and work harder than my peers. I jumped off the bed, took a bath, brushed my hair, and wrote new goals on a piece of paper. Then I tucked the paper in an old book and went to bed."

### 2. Identify the Problem Clearly

Don't assume readers know what the problem is. Name it.

**Example from social-media-detox.md:22**:
> "Are you running from the online demons or the demons in your mind?"

### 3. Provide a Framework or List

Give readers something they can use:
- Numbered practices (Zen Mode's 9 techniques)
- Decision frameworks (Ikigai in The Big Question)
- Checklists (What "practical" is NOT in Action-Faking)

### 4. Use Concrete Examples

Don't just give advice—show what it looks like in practice.

**Example from comfort-of-atomic-learning.md:26-30**:
> "Don't just be a graphics designer. Become a master at every tool until your work becomes art. Every stroke, color and gradient should have a story."

### 5. End with a Clear Summary

Every NDZ article ends with a **Summary** line that distills the main point.

**Examples**:
- "The genius is in the boring stuff" (zen-mode.md)
- "Focus on competence" (not-imposter-syndrome.md)
- "Create for your country. If the world wants it, business." (be-country-class.md)

## Tone and Style Guidelines

### ✅ Do This

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Be Direct" icon="bullseye">
    "You need a community you can call yours." Not: "Perhaps you might consider exploring the possibility of finding a community."
  </Card>

  <Card title="Challenge Assumptions" icon="chess">
    "Why do we want to be world-class so bad?" Don't accept conventional wisdom without questioning it.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Use 'You' Language" icon="user">
    Write directly to the reader. "If you can relate to this..." makes it personal and engaging.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Share Real Stories" icon="book">
    Personal anecdotes (like the school dance story in not-imposter-syndrome.md) make abstract concepts concrete.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

### ❌ Avoid This

<Warning>
  **No fluff or filler**: From action-faking.md:50-52: "Stop teasing your customers with what you can offer and do nothing to help them. It kills any opportunity for your business to thrive."
</Warning>

- Generic productivity advice without specific application
- Motivational content without actionable next steps
- Techniques that require expensive tools or resources
- Content that makes readers feel bad without providing a path forward
- "Life hack" content that's really just action-faking in disguise

## Submission Process

### Before You Write

1. **Read 5+ existing NDZ articles** to understand the voice and approach
2. **Ask yourself**: Does this challenge conventional wisdom or provide a fresh framework?
3. **Identify your audience**: Is this for freelancers? Remote workers? Beginners in a skill?

### Writing Your Draft

<Steps>
  <Step title="Write the summary first">
    What's the one-line takeaway? If you can't summarize it in one sentence, you're not clear enough yet.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Draft the outline">
    What's the hook? What's the framework? What are the examples? What's the call to action?
  </Step>

  <Step title="Write the first draft">
    Don't edit as you write. Get your ideas down.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Cut ruthlessly">
    Go through and delete anything that doesn't directly support your main point. "Less is More."
  </Step>

  <Step title="Add concrete examples">
    Replace abstract advice with specific scenarios or practices.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Formatting Your Article

Use this YAML frontmatter structure:

```markdown
+++
author = "Your Name"
title = "Article Title"
date = "YYYY-MM-DD"
description = "One-line description"
tags = [
  "Thoughts",
  // or "Tips" for how-to content
]
+++
```

**Article content starts here with the hook...**

**Use `<!--more-->`** after your first paragraph to create the excerpt.

### Submission Guidelines

<Accordion title="Article Length">
  Quality over quantity. Most NDZ articles are 500-1000 words. Some topics need more space, others need less. Ask: "Is every paragraph necessary?"
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Tags">
  Current tags used:
  - **Thoughts**: Essays, frameworks, mental models
  - **Tips**: How-to content, practical guides
  
  Choose the tag that best fits your content's primary purpose.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Voice">
  Write like you're talking to a friend who needs honest advice, not like you're giving a TED talk. NDZ content is conversational but not casual, direct but not harsh.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Editing">
  We edit for clarity and consistency with NDZ philosophy. Expect revisions. The goal is to make your ideas shine, not to change them.
</Accordion>

## Common Pitfalls

<Warning>
  **Action-faking in content**: Don't write an article about productivity that itself is full of filler. Practice what you preach.
</Warning>

### Articles We'll Reject

1. **Generic listicles**: "10 Ways to Be More Productive" without a fresh angle
2. **Motivational fluff**: Content that makes readers feel good but provides no actionable framework
3. **Get-rich-quick schemes**: Against NDZ's core philosophy of "honest ways to make money"
4. **Content that assumes resources**: Advice that requires expensive tools, courses, or access most readers don't have
5. **Victim mentality**: From about-ndz.md:62: "Complainers or people with a victim mindset" don't align with NDZ

## Questions to Ask Yourself

Before submitting, review your article against these questions:

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Is this actionable?">
    Can readers take a specific action after reading? What would they do differently tomorrow?
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Is this honest?">
    Are you sharing what actually works, including the hard parts? Or selling a dream?
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Is this minimal?">
    Could you cut 20% of the words and make it stronger? (Usually, yes.)
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Is this timeless?">
    Will this be relevant in 5 years? Or is it tied to a current trend?
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Does this respect the reader?">
    Are you assuming they're intelligent and capable? Or talking down to them?
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
