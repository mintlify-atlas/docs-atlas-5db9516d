## Deep Work & Focus

<Accordion title="Zen Mode: 9 Ways to Enter Deep Work">
  **The problem:** Most people think productivity means working 8 hours a day. Research shows the average worker is only productive for 2 hours and 53 minutes in an eight-hour day—about 36% effectiveness.
  
  **The solution:** Enter Zen mode—a state of deep work where boring tasks become interesting.
  
  **9 unique, low-budget techniques:**
  
  1. **Soundless media** - Mute your phone completely, even on social media
  2. **Single light source in a dark room** - Create isolation and immersion
  3. **First meal at noon** - Start with an empty stomach to avoid tiredness
  4. **Lofi sounds fasting** - Condition your mind with simple instrumental beats
  5. **Ultra minimalism** - Clean physical and digital workspace, close inactive tabs
  6. **Pinterest therapy** - Curate your feed to match your goals for daily motivation
  7. **Boring podcasts** - Listen to Lex Fridman, Naval Ravikant, Andrew Huberman
  8. **Watch classic series** - Suits, Billions, Peaky Blinders for leisure with lessons
  9. **Use a burner phone** - Stay reachable without smartphone distractions
  
  **How to measure productivity:** The guilt you feel in real time. The day feels perfect when you complete the most important task that lets you sleep without guilt.
  
  **Summary:** The genius is in the boring stuff.
  
  [Read the full guide →](https://journal.ndz.ng/zen-mode/)
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Social Media Without Losing Your Mind">
  **Don't quit social media—learn to use it right.** Here are 5 practices to live a more focused life:
  
  ### 1. Be Intentional With What You Consume
  
  Write down your interests: What do I love to see? When? From whom? Treat your social media circle like a cult. Don't follow random people. Follow role models and mentors, not every celebrity you see.
  
  ### 2. Train Your Mind to Ignore and Forget
  
  It's not real. Nobody is ugly on social media. When you catch yourself thinking "I wish I had that" or "God, when?", snap out of it: "This is not real; I'm doing my best."
  
  ### 3. Spend More Time in Deep Work
  
  The more time you spend doing what you love, the more tasteless social media becomes. Inform customers of your availability. Put your phone on DND during deep work.
  
  ### 4. Police Your Time With an Accountability Partner
  
  Set goals with a friend who shares your interests. Set a deadline. Anyone who defaults pays the price.
  
  ### 5. Build a Social-Media-Free Morning Routine
  
  Disconnect from the outside world within the first 2 hours of waking up. This protects your sanity and blocks obstacles that come later in the day.
  
  **Remember:** Digital minimalist systems don't work for everyone. Your mind is more powerful than any system created to save you from social media chaos.

</Accordion>

## Productivity & Execution

<Accordion title="Stop Action Faking: 7 Ways to Do Real Work">
  **What is action faking?** Working to impress people or avoiding real work at hand. It's when you:
  - Buy online "systems" instead of taking action
  - Print business cards when you should be building the business
  - Attend every seminar when you should start and learn from failures
  - Sharpen the knife instead of eating the chicken
  
  **Action faking is deadlier than procrastination and inconsistency combined.**
  
  ### What to Do Instead:
  
  **1. Eat the Frog** - When you're hearing the same thing over and over with different strategies, it's time to take action.
  
  **2. Know Who You Listen To** - When you have a $1000 goal, don't listen to a millionaire. Find someone close to what you want to be.
  
  **3. Quit the Fluff** - Get aggressive. Let prospects know you mean business. Stop teasing customers with what you can offer without helping them.
  
  **4. Make It Result-Oriented** - For every decision, ask: Is it result-driven? If you're doing it to look good while ignoring why you started, you're wasting time.
  
  **5. Build Strategies, Not Just Goals** - Goals are half-baked without strategies. Simply working on your idea doesn't cut it.
  
  **6. Take Some Minutes to Organize** - Examine where your efforts are going. Are you talking to convince people and doing nothing? Consuming content without taking action?
  
  **7. Stop Sharpening the Knife** - If your goal is to eat fried chicken but you keep sharpening the knife, you might end up with the world's sharpest knife.
  
  [Read the full article →](https://journal.ndz.ng/action-faking/)
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="How to Keep Winning Consciously">
  **The problem:** Being too comfortable makes you feel out of touch. You become unconscious of your wins and dwell in rejoicing, which leads to mistakes—and losses.
  
  **Three mindtras to be a conscious winner:**
  
  ### 1. Recognize That You've Won
  Take a moment to digest your win. This could be a 1-hour break, a day off work, dinner with your team, or an overnight party with friends. Note the little or big wins so you don't blur the lines between milestones.
  
  ### 2. Recognize That a Loss is Coming
  The mission hasn't ended. You will take a loss some hour, day, week, or month. You just won't know when. Keeping this in mind helps you stay conscious of the traps of over-rejoicing.
  
  ### 3. Recognize That the Day is Long
  As you go through rounds of wins and losses, know that it never ends until it's done. This prepares your mind for a tough journey without giving up.
  
  **Summary:** Play the long and short game, like a tai chi master trying to bend the wind.

</Accordion>

## Positioning & Strategy

<Accordion title="The Power of Positioning">
  **Success and failure can be measured by distance.** In Balogun Market, Lagos, the difference between high sales and low sales can be as simple as one busy crossroad. Buyers prioritize convenience and are risk-averse.
  
  **Key insights:**
  - Proximity to opportunities is wealth, power, and peace
  - If you spend your entire life in the same place, you'll likely become poorer than classmates who moved
  - Just 8-15 km separates poverty from wealth in Lagos (Balogun Market vs. Lekki/Victoria Island)
  - A physical change in distance gives you a mental shift
  
  **What to do:** If you feel stuck, like nothing is changing, it's time for a change. Rent a hotel for a few days, stay with a friend in another town, or move to a new state. The internet is also virtual space—if some parts feel restricting, change platforms. Be in control.
  
  **Summary:** Positioning is key.
